# AI Build vs Buy TCO Template

**AI Cost Volatility: Build vs Buy Analysis** (v10)

This Excel template helps Product Managers, finance teams, and leaders compare the **3-year Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** for four different approaches when AI is part of the equation.

Traditional build-vs-buy models break when AI enters the picture because **inference costs are volatile** — they scale with actual usage (interactions × tokens) rather than just number of seats.

### The Four Options Compared

1. **Traditional SaaS** – Predictable per-seat subscription costs
2. **Traditional Build** – High upfront investment, relatively fixed ongoing costs
3. **SaaS + AI** – Per-seat costs plus variable AI inference and related expenses
4. **Build + AI** – High upfront + ongoing ML operations + volatile AI inference costs

### What's Inside the Spreadsheet Tabs

- **ReadMe** – Purpose, instructions, and key insights
- **Assumptions** – Edit the **blue cells** with your own data (users, monthly interactions, tokens per interaction, token pricing, growth rates, usage multiplier, etc.)
- **Model** – Calculates annual costs and 3-year TCO with growth factors
- **Charts** – Visual comparison of total costs and annual growth over time
- **Documentation** – Explains the math, limitations, and strategic takeaways

### How to Use

1. Download and open the Excel file (`AI-Vs-BuildBuy-TCO-Template-v10.1523a.xlsx`)
2. Go to the **Assumptions** sheet and customize all **blue cells**
3. Pay special attention to the **AI Inference Parameters** section
4. Test different **Usage Multiplier** scenarios (1x, 5x, 10x) to see cost volatility
5. Review the **Model** and **Charts** sheets for results

**Important**: The default values are illustrative mid-market examples only. Replace them with your actual vendor quotes, usage data, and internal costs.

### Key Insight

Adding AI turns relatively predictable cost structures into highly variable ones. What looks cheap at baseline usage can become dramatically more expensive as adoption and interaction volume grow.

### Disclaimer

This is an **educational / illustrative model** designed to highlight AI cost dynamics. It is **not** a full financial model (no NPV, discounting, sensitivity analysis, or productivity benefits included). Use it as a starting point for discussions with finance and leadership.
